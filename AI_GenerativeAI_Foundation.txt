Artificial Intelligence is a field of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.
These tasks include learning from data, recognizing patterns, solving problems, making decisions, and understanding language.
 Its purpose is to simulate human-like reasoning and automate complex cognitive processes.

---

How Generative AI Works 

Generative AI works by learning patterns from massive datasets and building statistical models of how information is structured.
 Once trained, the model uses this knowledge to generate new content—such as text, images, audio, or video—by predicting what should come next based on learned relationships.
It operates through iterative sampling, adjusting each output step-by-step to produce coherent, realistic, and context-aware results.
This allows it to create content that resembles human creativity while relying strictly on learned patterns.

---

Popular Generative AI Models

Popular Generative AI models include Large Language Models like OpenAI’s GPT-3, GPT-4, and GPT-5, known for generating and understanding text.
Image models such as DALL-E and Stable Diffusion create visuals from descriptions.
Other notable models include Midjourney for artistic images and Google’s Gemini models for multimodal generation.

---

Limitations of Generative AI 

Generative AI still faces several limitations.
 It can inherit and amplify biases found in its training data, leading to unfair or inaccurate outputs.
Models may also produce “hallucinations,” inventing facts or details that are incorrect.
Their creativity is limited to recombining learned patterns rather than producing true original thought.
Ethical concerns include misinformation, deepfakes, and misuse. Additionally, training and running these models require significant computational resources, making development energy-intensive and expensive.
